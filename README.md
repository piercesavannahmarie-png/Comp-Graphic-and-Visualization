## Reflection

### How do I approach designing software?

When I approach designing software, I begin by reviewing the requirements and breaking the project into smaller steps. For this 3D scene project, I first selected a reference image and identified the objects I needed to recreate. Then I determined which primitive shapes could be combined to build those objects. Planning the scene layout ahead of time helped me stay organized and complete the work more efficiently.

### What new design skills has your work on the project helped you to craft?

This project helped me improve my ability to design scenes using multiple shapes, textures, and lighting techniques. I also learned how camera placement and object transformations affect how a scene appears. Matching my scene to a reference image strengthened my spatial reasoning and planning skills.

### What design process did you follow for your project work?

My design process included selecting a reference image, identifying the required objects, building each object step by step, and then refining the scene with textures and lighting. I adjusted object placement and scale throughout development to improve accuracy and realism.

### How could tactics from your design approach be applied in future work?

Breaking projects into smaller steps and testing changes frequently helped me stay organized and avoid errors. These strategies will be useful in future programming projects and graphics-based assignments.

### How do I approach developing programs?

I approach development by creating a working version first and then improving it through small adjustments. During this project, I focused on rendering objects correctly before adding textures and lighting. This made debugging easier and helped me build the scene more efficiently.

### What new development strategies did you use while working on your 3D scene?

One strategy I used was constructing complex objects from smaller primitive shapes. I also practiced applying textures and adjusting lighting values to improve realism. Testing frequently after each change helped me identify and fix problems quickly.

### How did iteration factor into your development?

Iteration played an important role throughout the project. I repeatedly adjusted object placement, lighting direction, and texture alignment to better match the reference image. Each revision improved the overall quality of the final scene.

### How has your approach to developing code evolved throughout the milestones?

At the beginning of the course, I focused mainly on displaying shapes correctly. As the milestones progressed, I became more comfortable working with transformations, textures, lighting, and camera movement. By the end of the project, I was able to combine these techniques into a complete 3D scene.
